About This Portal

This digital archive presents Charles Elmé Francatelli's The Modern Cook, first published in 1846 and here in its 25th edition of 1877. Francatelli, trained under the legendary Carême and later appointed Chief Cook and Maître d'Hôtel to Queen Victoria, produced one of the most comprehensive and authoritative cookbooks of the Victorian era.

Unlike many contemporary works, Francatelli's book is almost entirely recipes — no kitchen plans, no carving guides, no household advice. It is a pure culinary manual spanning French, Italian, German, and English cookery, designed for both the grandest establishments and private families.

About the Author

Charles Elmé Francatelli (1805–1876) was born in London to an Italian family and trained in France under Marie-Antoine Carême. He served as Chief Cook to Queen Victoria in 1840–1841, and subsequently worked at Crockford's Club, the Reform Club, the Coventry House, St. James's Hotel, and for the Prince and Princess of Wales.

The book includes thirteen authenticated bills of fare for "Her Majesty's Dinner" from 1841, providing an extraordinary window into royal dining. Francatelli also authored A Plain Cookery Book for the Working Classes (1852), demonstrating the same range as his contemporary Soyer.
